What is Strategy Builder?

Last updated · June 2025 · 4 min read

Strategy Builder is Melio's internal risk configuration tool. It lets the Risk Platform team define, version, and deploy the logic that determines how payments and organizations are evaluated for risk — without writing code.

Why it exists

Every payment that flows through Melio is assessed for risk. Historically, changing that assessment logic meant a code deployment — slow, error-prone, and requiring engineering involvement for every tweak. Strategy Builder removes that bottleneck.

With Strategy Builder, risk analysts can:

  • Write rule conditions in a visual query builder
  • Group rules into named strategies tied to a specific entity type
  • Test changes safely in shadow mode before going live
  • Roll back to any prior version from the version history

The two building blocks

Rules are the atomic unit. Each rule contains a set of conditions (e.g. "payment amount > $10,000 AND vendor country is not US") paired with an action and a risk decision ID. When a rule's conditions are met, its action fires.

Strategies are ordered collections of rules that apply to one entity type — either a payment or an organization. At evaluation time, Melio runs the strategy's rules in sequence and returns the first matching decision.

Deployment modes

Every strategy exists in one of three modes at any given time. This lets you iterate safely without affecting production traffic.

DraftWork in progress. Not evaluated against real traffic.
ShadowRuns alongside live, logs decisions, affects nothing.
LiveActive. Decisions are applied to real payments.

Who uses it

Strategy Builder is used by the Risk Platform team and risk analysts. Access is permission-controlled — if you need access, reach out to the Risk Platform team.
